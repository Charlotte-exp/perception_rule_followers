This study is made of three stages:

Stage 1: Die rolls

  • You will roll a die {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} times.
  • After each roll, you report the number you rolled.
  • The number you report is the number of points you will be paid for that roll.
  • 1 point is worth {{ C.conversion }}.

Stage 2: Interaction with another participant in the study

  • After the {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} die rolls, you are matched with another participant from the same study.
  • You will the number of times the other participant reported the correct number, and they will see the number of times you reported the correct number. {{ if player.treatment == 'rating' }}
  • Based on this information, you will rate their behaviour.
  • You will be paid a {{ C.bonus }} points bonus if your rating is the same as the most common answer for that participant. {{ endif }} {{ if player.treatment == 'TG' }}
  • Based on this information, you will interact in a two stage task with them.
  • You will be paid two separate bonus points based on that interaction ranging from 0 to 9 points. {{ endif }} {{ if player.treatment == 'DG' }}
  • Based on this information, you will interact in a two stage task with them.
  • You will the opportunity to earn another bonus based on that interaction ranging from 0 to 9 points. {{ endif }}

Stage 3: Extra interaction with a participant from another study

  • After stage 2 is over, you are matched with a new participant from a different study.
  • The other participant will now see only one of your randomly selected die roll, as well as the number you reported for that roll.
  • Based on this information, you will interact in an extra task with them.
  • You will the opportunity to earn another bonus based on that interaction ranging from 0 to 9 points.

To summarise: You get three bonus, one from the die number you report, two from the interaction in stage 2, and one from the interaction in stage 3. Because the 3rd participant will be matched with you in a later study, you will receive both bonuses later.
