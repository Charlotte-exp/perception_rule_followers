This study is made of three stages:

Stage 1: Die rolls

  • You will roll a die in the game {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} times.
  • After each roll, you must report the number you rolled.
  • The number you report is the number of points you will be paid for that roll.
  • 1 point is worth {{ C.conversion }}.
  • Only one of the {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} dice will be randomly selected for payment

Stage 2: Interaction with another participant in the study

  • After the {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} die rolls, you are matched with another participant from this same study.
  • This participant will see the number of times you reported the correct number from stage 1, and you will interact together in stage 2. {{ if player.treatment == 'TG' }}
  • First, the other participant will choose how many points to send to you after having seen how many times you reported the correct die.
  • Then, these points are tripled by the computer so that you get 3x as much as they sent you.
  • Finally, you have to decide how many of those tripled points you wish to keep to yourself and how many you want to send back, if any.
  • The points you kept will be paid to you as the stage 2 bonus.
  • Important: to decide how many points to send to you in the first place, the other participant will only know how many times you reported the correct die. {{ endif }} {{ if player.treatment == 'DG' }}
  • In stage 2, the other participant is given {{ C.DG_points }} and must choose how many of these points to send to you after having seen how many times you reported the correct die.
  • The points you receive from the other participant will be paid to you as the stage 2 bonus.
  • Important: to decide how many points to send to you in the first place, the other participant will only know how many times you reported the correct die. {{ endif }} {{ if player.treatment == 'rating' }}
  • In stage 2, the other participant must rate how trustworthy you seem after having seen how many times you reported the correct die.
  • Important: to decide how many points to send to you in the first place, the other participant will only know how many times you reported the correct die. {{ endif }}

Stage 3: Extra interaction with a participant from another study

  • After stage 2 is over, you are matched with a new participant from a different study.
  • The other participant will now see only one of your randomly selected die roll, as well as the number you reported for that roll.
  • Based on this information, you will interact in a task with them and earn a bonus based on the outcome of that task.

The bonuses from each stage will be paid once the whole study is over.
